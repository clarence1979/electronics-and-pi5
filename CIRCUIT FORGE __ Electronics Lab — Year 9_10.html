// Mission Brief

You already live inside circuits

You can't see them, but billions of tiny switches and wires are working for you right now. Understanding how they work turns you from a user of technology into a maker of it.

📱

Your phone

Modern cars

Game consoles

🛰️

Satellites

Hardened circuits survive radiation and -150°C to keep GPS and the internet alive.

// The Three Quantities

Voltage, Current & Resistance — the water trick

Electricity is invisible, so engineers picture it as water in pipes. Drag the sliders and watch how the three quantities push and pull on each other. This single mental model unlocks almost everything.

Voltage (V)
9 V
pump pressure
Resistance (Ω)
470 Ω
pipe narrowness
Current (A)
19 mA
flow rate
9 V
470 Ω
Ohm's Law  →  I = V ÷ R  =  9 ÷ 470 = 0.019 A

Voltage is the push. Resistance slows the flow. Current is what actually moves. Raise voltage → more flow. Add resistance → less flow. That's Ohm's Law, the most useful equation in electronics.

// Component Dossier

Meet your toolkit

Each component does one job. Learn the symbol and the job, and you can read any circuit diagram on Earth.

Battery / Cell

The pump. Provides voltage that pushes current around the loop.

📍 AA cell = 1.5 V · 9 V block · car battery = 12 V

source

Resistor

Limits current to a safe level. Without one, sensitive parts burn out.

📍 Protects LEDs, sets brightness, divides voltage

protection

LED

Light Emitting Diode. Glows when current flows — but only one way (it has polarity).

📍 Indicators, screens, traffic lights, your TV remote

output · diode

Switch

A controllable gap. Closed = current flows. Open = circuit broken, no flow.

📍 Light switches, buttons, keyboards

control

Lamp / Bulb

Turns electrical energy into light and heat. Brighter with more current.

📍 Torches, headlights, indicators

output · load

Wire / Jumper

A path with (almost) no resistance. Connects components into a complete loop.

📍 Every circuit ever made needs these

connection

// Skill Challenges

Missions

Try these in the Lab above. Each one teaches a real circuit-design rule. Tick them off as you go.

01

First Light

Wire a battery → resistor → LED so it glows. If you skip the resistor, watch what happens to the LED.

complete circuit · current limiting
02

The Switch

Add a switch so you can turn your LED on and off. Toggle it while RUN is active.

control · open vs closed
03

Two in a Row

Put two resistors in series. Use the multimeter — does total resistance go up or down?

series resistance
04

Side by Side

Wire two LEDs in parallel off one battery. Notice they each get the full voltage.

parallel circuits
05

Right Way Round

Flip an LED so it points backwards. Run it. Why does nothing happen? (LEDs have polarity!)

diodes · polarity
06

Power Up

Build a circuit with a motor or buzzer. Measure the current it draws compared to an LED.

loads · power
